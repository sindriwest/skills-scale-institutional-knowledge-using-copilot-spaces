# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and help the team adopt continuous improvement practices. They serve the team by coaching Agile principles and shielding the team from outside distractions.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that slow the team down
- Coach the team on Agile and Scrum principles
- Foster a culture of transparency, inspection, and adaptation
- Track team velocity and support sprint goal alignment

### Goals
- Maximize team throughput by eliminating waste and blockers
- Build self-organizing, high-performing teams
- Ensure ceremonies are effective and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and retrospective facilitation
- Impediment logs and escalation summaries
- Team health metrics and velocity reports

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on delivery timelines and escalates cross-team blockers
- **Product Manager**: Coordinates backlog readiness and sprint goal alignment
- **Developers**: Coaches on Agile practices and removes technical or process impediments

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for the user experience and visual design of the product. They ensure that features are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs and brand standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Collaborate with Product Managers on requirements and acceptance criteria
- Hand off final design assets to Developers with clear specifications
- Validate designs through usability testing and stakeholder review

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent user experiences
- Reduce rework by involving design early in the development cycle
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with stakeholders
- Handoff documentation and annotated design files
- Collaboration in sprint ceremonies to clarify UX requirements

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Translates business requirements into user-centered designs
- **Developers**: Provides design specifications and reviews implemented UI for fidelity
- **Project Manager**: Communicates design milestone status and flags scope changes
- **QA/Testing**: Reviews implemented UI against design specs during acceptance testing

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers ensure that infrastructure and deployment pipelines are robust, automated, and scalable. They bridge the gap between development and operations to support continuous delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD workflows and deployment pipelines
- Monitor production systems for performance, reliability, and security
- Assist with release management, rollback strategies, and incident response
- Enforce security and compliance requirements in deployment processes
- Automate infrastructure provisioning and environment management

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable software delivery
- Minimize downtime and accelerate incident resolution
- Ensure infrastructure scales with product and team needs

### Typical Communication
- Deployment readiness reports and post-incident reviews
- Infrastructure documentation and runbooks
- CI/CD pipeline status updates and alerts

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Supports build, test, and deployment tooling; reviews infrastructure-as-code contributions
- **Project Manager**: Communicates release readiness, deployment schedules, and operational risks
- **Product Manager**: Informs on infrastructure constraints that may affect feature timelines
- **QA/Testing**: Maintains staging and test environments to support QA workflows

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts act as a bridge between business needs and the technical team. They ensure that requirements are well-defined, actionable, and traceable to business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and analyze business requirements from stakeholders
- Document user stories, process flows, and acceptance criteria
- Validate proposed solutions against business objectives and success metrics
- Perform gap analysis between current and desired states
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder alignment sessions

### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver measurable business value
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements to minimize rework
- Maintain clear traceability between business goals and delivered features

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story refinement sessions
- Stakeholder alignment meetings and workshop outputs
- Business impact assessments and acceptance sign-offs

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on roadmap priorities and translates business needs into product requirements
- **Project Manager**: Provides requirements clarity to support accurate planning and risk identification
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements during implementation and validates delivered features
- **Stakeholders**: Elicits requirements, manages expectations, and obtains sign-off on acceptance criteria
